Ben, a Veteran Interpreter and Translator

I've been a professional interpreter and translaor for more than five years, helping many in bridging the language barrier. I would like to share a bit about my experience and what I've learned.

How I Started

The beginnings of my career as a professional interpreter and translator was not something I entirely expected, but in hindsight, it was something that I grew into.

When I was still in school, I oftentimes found myself helping family and friends who had trouble communicating in English, well, communicate with English. Someone being able to receive help in overcoming the language barrier is a given. Essential even. If I could be the one to do it, then of course I would.

Whether it was translating documents into written Chinese or relaying the contents of phone calls back into Cantonese or Mandarin, I continued to help without considering what I provided was a service. Something valuable. A skill that many would need. and maybe aren't fortunate enough to know someone who can help, or be at a position where help was readily available.

So with the skills I gained by just being myself and helping others, there came a time when I had a thought. Maybe I could apply my abilities even further, put them to even better use. Maybe, there was something more that I could do. With that, I decided to try my hand at being a professional, and join in others in providing a service that could help many more.
